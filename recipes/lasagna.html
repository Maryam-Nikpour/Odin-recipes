lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a type of Italian dish made with layers of flat, wide pasta, sauce, cheese, and sometimes meat or vegetables.Lasagna is often considered a comfort food and is a popular dish for family dinners, potlucks, and special occasions. The dish can be made ahead of time and reheated, making it a convenient option for hosting dinner parties or large gatherings. Overall, lasagna is a rich, hearty, and filling dish that is beloved by many people around the world.Traditionally, the sauce used in lasagna is a meat-based tomato sauce, although vegetarian options such as a tomato and basil sauce or a cream-based sauce are also common. The cheese used is typically mozzarella, but Parmesan, ricotta, or other types of cheese may also be used. The filling ingredients can vary, but popular options include ground beef, sausage, spinach, and mushrooms.

Ingredients

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 jar (24 oz) of marinara sauce
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 container (15 oz) of ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper
  • Olive oil for cooking

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C)
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large pan, heat a small amount of olive oil over medium heat. Add the ground beef and cook until browned, breaking it up into small pieces as it cooks. Drain any excess fat.
  4. Add the marinara sauce and water to the pan with the beef and stir to combine. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. In a separate bowl, mix together the ricotta cheese, egg, salt, and pepper.
  6. In a 9x13 inch baking dish, spread a thin layer of the meat sauce on the bottom. Arrange 3 lasagna noodles on top of the sauce, overlapping if necessary.
  7. Spread half of the ricotta mixture over the noodles, then sprinkle with 1 cup of shredded mozzarella cheese. Repeat the layering with another layer of sauce, noodles, ricotta mixture, and mozzarella cheese.
  8. Top with a final layer of noodles and spread the remaining meat sauce on top. Sprinkle with the remaining mozzarella cheese and grated Parmesan cheese.
  9. Cover the dish with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  10. Let the lasagna cool for 10 minutes before serving. Enjoy!
